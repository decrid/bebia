# Bebia mobile product research

Date: 2026-04-14

## What modern mobile apps should do well

- Deliver value in the first session with clear next actions, not just navigation.
- Show strong trust signals around privacy, data control, and what AI can or cannot do.
- Reduce effort with one-tap shortcuts, helpful empty states, and progressive setup.
- Keep the home screen focused on "what matters now" instead of raw history only.
- Support retention through personalization, editable history, and contextual guidance.

## What this means for Bebia

- Home should feel like a daily assistant, not only a tracker.
- Onboarding should explain outcome, trust, and setup priority in simple language.
- Family sharing should feel understandable and safe, even before full cloud sync exists.
- Empty states should tell the parent what to do next and why it matters.

## Changes implemented

- Added a richer home overview with daily pulse, setup progress, and quick actions.
- Added onboarding guidance focused on privacy, data control, and AI as support.
- Kept the product direction local-first and parent-controlled, which fits the current app architecture.

## External references reviewed

- Android app quality guidelines: core value, UX, technical quality, privacy/security.
- Material guidance for mobile clarity and standard navigation patterns.
- General UX guidance around personalization, onboarding, and trust signals from current industry sources.
